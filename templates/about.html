The Genesis

Regressionary was engineered to solve a specific academic friction point: the disconnect between raw econometric theory and modern user experience.

Traditional statistical software is incredibly powerful but often gatekept by steep learning curves and archaic interfaces. Conversely, simple online calculators provide outputs without enforcing the rigorous methodology—like OLS assumptions and hypothesis testing—required for actual economic analysis.

This mainframe bridges that gap. It is an operating system for learning econometrics, forcing the user to validate their model before interpreting their results.

The Architect

Designed and developed by an economics undergraduate and aspiring software developer. This application serves as a functional intersection of demography, financial theory, and full-stack web development.

The UI/UX draws heavy inspiration from minimalist hardware aesthetics, focusing on high contrast, stark typography, and distraction-free data visualization.
